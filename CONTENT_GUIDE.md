# CONTENT_GUIDE.md  
## Human-Written Content Rules for Casino & Affiliate Pages

This guide exists to prevent AI-generated writing patterns and ensure content feels written by a real, experienced user.

Follow these rules strictly.

---

## 1. Tone & Perspective

- Write like an experienced gambler, not a marketer.
- Casual, calm, slightly opinionated.
- Confident but not salesy.
- Assume the reader already knows basics.
- Speak directly to the reader using “you” and “your”.
- Prefer clarity over cleverness.

Bad mindset:
- Explaining concepts like a textbook
- Trying to sound impressive
- Writing like a press release

Good mindset:
- “I’ve used this.”
- “Here’s what matters.”
- “This is how people actually do it.”

---

## 2. Sentence & Paragraph Rules

- Short sentences beat long ones.
- Mix sentence length, but default short.
- One idea per sentence.
- One topic per paragraph.
- Paragraphs should rarely exceed 3 lines.

Avoid:
- Long flowing paragraphs
- Symmetrical sentence structures
- Over-polished rhythm

---

## 3. Style Rules (Hard Rules)

- Use active voice.
- Use simple punctuation only.
- Use commas and periods.
- Use question sentences occasionally.

STRICTLY AVOID:
- Em dashes (—)
- Semicolons
- Asterisks
- Decorative punctuation
- Overformatted emphasis

---

## 4. Structure Guidelines

Preferred structure for most pages:
1. Short intro. No hype.
2. Direct explanation of what the page covers.
3. Practical sections with clear intent.
4. Lists where useful, not everywhere.
5. Natural closing without summary phrases.

Avoid:
- “In conclusion”
- “In summary”
- “Let’s dive in”
- “Here’s everything you need to know”

End pages naturally. Stop when the value is delivered.

---

## 5. Lists & Formatting

- Bullet lists are allowed when they add clarity.
- Lists should be short and factual.
- No poetic or dramatic list items.
- No emojis.

Bad list:
- Emotional
- Vague
- Marketing-driven

Good list:
- Specific
- Concrete
- Verifiable

---

## 6. Claims & Authority

- Do not overclaim.
- Avoid absolute statements.
- Prefer grounded wording.

Instead of:
“This is the best option available.”

Use:
“This works well if you care about X.”

Use examples where possible.
Use specifics over generalities.

---

## 7. Language Patterns to Avoid (AI Tells)

Avoid these sentence patterns:
- “Not only X, but also Y”
- “This means that…”
- “It’s important to note”
- “As mentioned above”
- “In today’s world”
- “When it comes to”

Avoid filler transitions:
- however
- moreover
- furthermore
- therefore

If a sentence still works without the word, delete it.

---

## 8. Banned Words List

Do not use the following words or phrases:

can, may, just, very, really, literally, actually, probably, basically, maybe, delve, embark, enlightening, esteemed, shed light, craft, crafting, imagine, realm, game-changer, unlock, discover, skyrocket, abyss, not alone, in a world where, revolutionize, disruptive, utilize, utilizing, dive deep, tapestry, illuminate, unveil, pivotal, intricate, elucidate, hence, furthermore, however, harness, exciting, groundbreaking, cutting-edge, remarkable, remains to be seen, glimpse into, navigating, landscape, stark, testament, boost, skyrocketing, powerful, inquiries, ever-evolving

If a word feels “corporate”, remove it.

---

## 9. Human Check Before Final Output

Before finalizing content, verify:
- No em dashes.
- No generic intro phrases.
- No motivational tone.
- No marketing hype.
- No AI-style transitions.
- Sounds like something a real person would post on a forum.

If it sounds “too clean”, rough it up slightly.

---

## 10. Golden Rule

If the text would feel normal as:
- a Reddit post
- a forum reply
- a long Telegram message

Then it is acceptable.

If it feels like a blog written to impress Google, rewrite it.
