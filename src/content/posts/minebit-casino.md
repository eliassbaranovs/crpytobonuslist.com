It is widely considered a low-tier regulator.

Anjouan licenses are relatively easy and inexpensive to obtain, and the authority provides minimal oversight and virtually no effective player dispute resolution services. If a player has a disagreement with the casino over payments or bonus terms, there is little to no recourse through the regulator. This stands in stark contrast to top-tier authorities like the Malta Gaming Authority (MGA) or the UK Gambling Commission (UKGC), which enforce strict standards for fairness, security, and responsible gambling, and provide robust channels for complaint mediation. The choice of an Anjouan license is a major red flag and indicates that players must rely solely on the casino's own reputation, which is limited due to its recent launch in 2025.
