### Licensing and Security

A casino's license is the foundation of its trustworthiness, and Dexsport's choice of regulator is a critical point of analysis. The platform is operated by Dexapp LTD and holds a license from the Anjouan Gaming Authority (license number ALSI-202508043-FI2), based in the Union of Comoros. Anjouan has emerged as a licensing jurisdiction following regulatory reforms in Curacao, attracting operators seeking a faster and less stringent licensing process.

Compared to top-tier regulators like the Malta Gaming Authority (MGA) or the UK Gambling Commission (UKGC), an Anjouan license offers significantly less player protection. Dispute resolution mechanisms are limited, and the regulatory oversight is not as rigorous. This means that in the event of a conflict with the casino, players have very little recourse. This is a major consideration for anyone prioritizing safety and fair treatment.

On the security front, Dexsport has taken steps to validate its technical infrastructure. The platform's smart contracts underwent an audit by CertiK in January 2022 and have also been reviewed by the security firm Pessimistic and ECHELON. While these audits provide some assurance of technical soundness, it is worth noting that the CertiK audit highlighted some centralization issues that were not fully resolved. The platform also employs provably fair mechanics for its in-house games, adding a layer of transparency.

The no-KYC policy is a double-edged sword. For privacy advocates, it is a key feature, allowing for anonymous gambling without submitting personal documents. However, the absence of mandatory identity verification also raises concerns regarding Anti-Money Laundering (AML) compliance and the prevention of underage gambling.
