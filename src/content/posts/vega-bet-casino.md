## Licensing and Security

When it comes to licensing and security, Vega.bet Casino operates in a gray area that should give players pause. The casino is licensed and regulated by the Anjouan Gaming Board, based in the Union of Comoros. This is not one of the industry's top-tier regulatory bodies. Licenses from authorities like the Malta Gaming Authority (MGA) or the UK Gambling Commission (UKGC) are held in high regard because they enforce strict standards for player protection, game fairness, and operational transparency. An Anjouan license does not carry the same weight and offers a lower level of player recourse in case of disputes.

The security concerns are amplified by the lack of public audits for its Random Number Generator (RNG) and Return to Player (RTP) percentages. An RNG is the core software that ensures the outcomes of casino games are truly random and fair. Independent testing agencies like eCOGRA or iTech Labs regularly audit this software to certify its integrity. Vega.bet has not provided any such certification. This means players have to trust the casino and its game providers to be fair without any independent verification. This one does not check out.

Similarly, the absence of a publicly audited RTP report is another red flag. This report details the actual percentage of wagered money that a casino has paid back to players over time. It is a key indicator of a casino's fairness and transparency. While the individual games from reputable providers have their own theoretical RTPs, a casino-wide audit provides a layer of accountability. The lack of these fundamental trust signals is a serious issue. While the casino likely uses standard SSL encryption to protect user data, the weaknesses in its regulatory oversight and fairness verification are too significant to ignore.
