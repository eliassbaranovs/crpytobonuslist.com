## Deposits and Withdrawals

CloudBet’s banking system is built for the crypto-native user, offering exceptional flexibility and a vast array of supported digital assets. The platform’s approach caters to both seasoned crypto holders and newcomers looking to use fiat-to-crypto on-ramps.

The casino supports over 40 cryptocurrencies, covering all major assets and many popular altcoins. This hybrid model bridges the gap between traditional finance and the crypto world, though the platform's core functionality remains crypto-centric. Players who prefer using specific altcoins will find options like **[Solana Casinos](/solana-casinos/)** well-represented here.

### Withdrawal Speed

CloudBet processes crypto withdrawals internally within 24 hours. Once approved, the transaction speed depends solely on the blockchain network's congestion. Coins like SOL and XRP typically confirm in under five minutes, while BTC and ETH can take longer during peak times. However, this advertised speed comes with a critical condition: Know Your Customer (KYC) verification.

The casino triggers a mandatory KYC check for withdrawals exceeding approximately $2,200 per day or if any account activity raises AML flags. This process, handled by Sumsub, requires submitting a government-issued ID and proof of address. Verification typically takes an additional 24 to 48 hours. Therefore, a player's first significant withdrawal could realistically take up to 72 hours (24 hours for internal review + 48 hours for KYC) before the funds are even sent to the blockchain. This delay is a significant factor for players expecting instant payouts. For those who prioritize speed above all, exploring our list of **[Fast Withdrawal Crypto Casinos](/fast-withdrawal-crypto-casinos/)** may provide better alternatives.

### Fees

CloudBet states that it does not charge a platform fee for withdrawals. Instead, it applies a flat network fee to cover the blockchain transaction cost, which is standard practice. However, the terms and conditions reserve the right to apply a handling fee of up to 5% on both deposits and withdrawals. This is a considerable potential cost that can significantly impact a player's winnings and is a notable drawback compared to competitors who offer fee-free transactions. Players should be aware of this possibility before committing large sums.

## Licensing and Security

CloudBet is operated by Halcyon Super Holdings B.V. and holds a license from the Curaçao Gaming Authority (CGA) with the number OGL/2024/328/0599. This license is part of the new framework established by Curaçao's government to bring regulation directly under a state authority, moving away from the old master license system. This is a positive step towards better oversight and accountability.

However, it is important to place this license in context. The Curaçao Gaming Authority, even under its new structure, does not offer the same level of player protection, dispute resolution, or stringent operational standards as top-tier regulators like the Malta Gaming Authority (MGA) or the UK Gambling Commission (UKGC). Players have fewer official channels for recourse in case of a dispute. Despite this, CloudBet's long operational history since 2013 provides a strong, informal record of reliability.

On the security front, the platform uses standard SSL encryption to protect user data and financial transactions. A major trust factor is the use of provably fair technology for its in-house "Originals" games. This cryptographic method allows players to independently verify that the outcome of each game round was random and not manipulated. ## Responsible Gambling

CloudBet's approach to responsible gambling is surprisingly limited for a platform of its age and stature. The available tools are minimal and fall significantly short of the industry standard, which is a major area of concern. Players have very few options to proactively manage their gambling habits directly through their account settings.

The casino offers two primary tools: a Cool-Off Period and Self-Exclusion. The Cool-Off Period is a mandatory 24-hour break that locks all wagering, deposits, and withdrawals. Self-Exclusion is a more serious step, allowing players to block access to their account for temporary periods (one week, one month, six months, one year) or permanently. This can be activated without needing to contact customer support.

Critically, CloudBet fails to provide essential financial control tools. There are no options to set deposit limits, loss limits, or wager limits. The platform also lacks session time limits and reality-check reminders, which are designed to help players stay aware of how long they have been playing. This absence of proactive controls places the entire burden of responsible play on the user and is a significant weakness.
