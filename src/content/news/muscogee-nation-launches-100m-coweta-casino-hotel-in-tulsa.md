# Muscogee Nation Launches $100M Coweta Casino Hotel in Tulsa

## The Core Update

Muscogee (Creek) Nation has unveiled a $100 million casino-hotel in Tulsa, Oklahoma. The Coweta Casino Hotel, operated by Muscogee Nation Gaming Enterprises (MNGE), replaces the tribe's former Coweta casino. The facility, located in the Tulsa suburb of Coweta, features a 35,000 square foot casino with 750 advanced gaming machines.

## The Numbers

The Coweta Casino Hotel's construction supported approximately 501 jobs, generating $22.6 million in income. The total economic impact reached $132.5 million. Post-opening, the casino-hotel adds 596 direct and indirect jobs, contributing an estimated $27.5 million in new annual income. This development is expected to significantly bolster the local economy.

## Market Impact

This strategic move by the Muscogee Nation aims to intensify competition with neighboring tribal casinos, including those operated by the Cherokee and Choctaw Nations. Muscogee Nation Gaming Enterprises COO Andy Langston emphasized the tribe's deliberate expansion into Coweta to strengthen its foothold in the regional market.

## Player Impact

For patrons, Coweta Casino Hotel promises an enhanced gaming experience with its state-of-the-art machines. The facility's modern amenities are likely to attract a broader audience, positioning it as a key player in the crowded Oklahoma tribal casino market.

## What Happens Next

The Muscogee Nation's investment reflects a calculated effort to enhance its market position. The tribe's continued partnership with local businesses and tourism bodies underscores its commitment to regional growth. Observers will be watching how this new entrant affects the dynamics among Oklahoma's tribal casinos.
