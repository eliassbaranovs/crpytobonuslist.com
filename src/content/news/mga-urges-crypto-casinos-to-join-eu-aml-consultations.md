# MGA Urges Crypto Casinos to Join EU AML Consultations

## The Details
The Malta Gaming Authority (MGA) is urging its licensees to join EU consultations on anti-money laundering (AML) standards. The European Union’s Anti-Money Laundering Authority (AMLA) has launched this initiative. It's centered on three draft Regulatory Technical Standards (RTS). These standards focus on customer due diligence as per Article 28(1) of the Anti-Money Laundering Regulation (EU 2024/1624). The consultations aim to tighten AML measures across the gaming sector.

## Market & Player Impact
Crypto casinos might face stricter compliance procedures soon. The new AML standards could lead to enhanced KYC requirements. This means players might need to provide more detailed personal information. The changes could limit anonymous play and affect how deposits and withdrawals are processed. Key regions impacted include all countries within the EU.

## What Happens Next
1. Casinos should review the draft RTS documents in detail.
2. Licensees need to prepare for potential updates in compliance protocols.
3. Players should expect more stringent verification processes.
4. Industry stakeholders must submit feedback during the consultation period.
5. Monitor announcements from the MGA for further guidance.

## Key Facts
- Regulator involved: Malta Gaming Authority (MGA)
- Consultation launched by: EU's Anti-Money Laundering Authority (AMLA)
- Relevant regulation: EU 2024/1624 Article 28(1)
- Regions impacted: European Union
- Focus areas: Customer due diligence, business relationship criteria

## Industry Context
- **Entity**: EU Anti-Money Laundering Authority
- **Market Segment**: Gaming Sector
- **Precedent**: Similar AML updates were considered in 2021
- **Status**: Active consultation phase

## Bottom Line
According to recent filings, the upcoming AML regulations could reshape compliance procedures in crypto casinos. The regulatory landscape is tightening, signaling more in-depth player verification and data collection. Industry sources confirm the need for stakeholders to actively participate in shaping these standards.
