# Muscogee Nation's $100M Casino-Hotel Shakes Up Tulsa Market

The Muscogee Nation has launched a $100 million casino-hotel in Tulsa, Oklahoma. The Coweta Casino Hotel, operated by Muscogee Nation Gaming Enterprises, replaces the tribe's former Coweta casino with a new 35,000-square-foot facility. The casino features 750 advanced gaming machines, setting the stage for heightened competition among Oklahoma's tribal casinos.

## The Economic Numbers

The construction phase of the Coweta Casino Hotel generated significant economic benefits. According to Indian Gaming, the project supported 501 jobs and contributed $22.6 million in income, leading to a total economic impact of $132.5 million. Post-opening, the casino expects to create 596 direct and indirect jobs, adding $27.5 million in annual income.

## Competitive Market Context

The Muscogee Nation's new venture enters a crowded market dominated by tribal casinos like those operated by the Cherokee and Choctaw Nations. Andy Langston, COO of MNGE, emphasized the strategic nature of this $100 million investment. The tribe aims to leverage its new facility to carve out a competitive edge in the regional gaming landscape.

## Local Economic Boost

The Coweta Casino Hotel's operations are anticipated to benefit local businesses significantly. Joe Matlock from BancFirst Coweta highlighted the 250+ permanent jobs as a substantial boost to small businesses in the area. Coweta Mayor Naomi Hogue and Tulsa Regional Tourism's Renee McKenney both praised the Muscogee Nation for its partnership and economic contributions to the region.

## What Happens Next

The Muscogee Nation plans to monitor the casino's performance closely as it competes with other tribal entities. The expansion into Coweta signifies a calculated move designed to solidify their presence in the lucrative Oklahoma gambling market. The tribe's leadership remains confident in their strategic direction and the potential for future growth.
