# UK Gambling Commission Eyes Cryptocurrency Payments in Regulatory Shift

The UK Gambling Commission (UKGC) is exploring the integration of cryptocurrency payments within its licensed gambling sector. This announcement was made by Tim Miller, Executive Director of the UKGC, during the Betting and Gaming Council's Annual General Meeting. The move aligns with the growing interest among British gamblers in using cryptocurrencies and aims to address illegal gambling concerns.

## The Numbers

The UK online gambling market faces a significant challenge with illegal operations. In 2025, unlicensed operators accounted for 9% of Britain's £8.2 billion online gambling market, a sharp increase from 2% in 2022. This equates to £379 million siphoned off in just the first half of 2025. The math doesn't lie: these numbers underscore the urgent need for regulatory changes.

## Regulatory Context

The UKGC's exploration of cryptocurrency payments is tied to the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (Cryptoassets) Regulations 2025. If passed, this legislation will bring cryptoassets under the Financial Conduct Authority's (FCA) regulatory umbrella by October 25, 2027. This framework is essential for ensuring secure and compliant cryptocurrency transactions in the gambling sector.

## Market Impact

Integrating cryptocurrencies could reshape the UK's gambling landscape. With the FCA's oversight, licensed operators might gain competitive advantages by offering crypto payments, attracting tech-savvy players. However, the shift could also challenge operators to adapt to new regulatory and technological demands swiftly.

## Player Impact

For players, the potential for cryptocurrency payments means more options and convenience. The ability to gamble with crypto could appeal to those interested in digital currencies' anonymity and efficiency. ## What Happens Next

The UKGC has tasked the Industry Forum with progressing this initiative sensibly. While no specific timeline is set, the exploration phase is underway, with significant developments likely as the 2027 deadline approaches. As regulations evolve, players and operators should stay informed on compliance and legal requirements.
