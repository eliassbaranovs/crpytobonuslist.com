# UKGC Considers Crypto Payments in Gambling

The UK Gambling Commission (UKGC) is exploring the integration of cryptocurrency payments into the UK’s licensed gambling sector. This initiative was disclosed by Tim Miller, the Executive Director of the UKGC, at the Betting and Gaming Council's Annual General Meeting.

## UKGC's Crypto Exploration

Tim Miller highlighted the growing interest among punters for cryptocurrency gambling and acknowledged the current illegal market's expansion. Illegal gambling now constitutes 9% of the UK’s £8.2 billion online gambling market, according to UKGC data. Unlicensed operations siphoned off £379 million in the first half of 2025 alone.

The UKGC is considering how cryptocurrencies could be integrated into licensed gambling sites. While no specific timeline has been given, the Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (Cryptoassets) Regulations 2025 could be pivotal. This legislation, if passed, would bring cryptoassets under the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) by October 2027.

## Regulatory Context

The Financial Services and Markets Act 2025 aims to establish a regulatory framework for cryptoassets in the UK. The FCA's oversight is crucial for the UKGC's consideration of crypto as a legal payment method. This move aligns with global trends where regulations are easing for cryptocurrency gambling.

Currently, there are no licensed crypto gambling sites in the UK. However, players often access international casinos that do not geo-block UK residents. The UKGC aims to curb illegal gambling by potentially offering regulated crypto payment options.
