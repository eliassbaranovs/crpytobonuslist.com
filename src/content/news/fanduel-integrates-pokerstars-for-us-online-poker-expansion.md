# FanDuel Integrates PokerStars for US Online Poker Expansion

FanDuel has announced its entry into the US online poker market through a strategic integration with PokerStars. Both brands are owned by Flutter Entertainment. This move links PokerStars' operations with FanDuel’s platform, requiring PokerStars' US players to access their accounts through FanDuel.

## The Core Update

FanDuel will soon offer online poker to its US customers by integrating PokerStars into its platform. Players in Michigan, New Jersey, and Pennsylvania will benefit from shared player pools. This marks a significant shift in how PokerStars users will access their accounts, as they must now log in via FanDuel.

## The Numbers

The integration facilitates shared player pools across three states, enhancing traffic. This aims to improve tournament guarantees. Players from Pennsylvania joining Michigan and New Jersey will likely see larger prize pools and more active tables.

## Regulatory Context

PokerStars’ entry into Pennsylvania’s shared liquidity pool with Michigan and New Jersey addresses long-standing regulatory hurdles. This move aligns with existing legal frameworks, as all three states already permit online poker and shared liquidity.

## Player Impact

Players will need to create FanDuel accounts to access PokerStars offerings. New account holders will receive fresh welcome bonuses. The transition means that PokerStars' current software will be phased out, although poker will still be available on a separate app.

## Market Impact

This integration allows PokerStars to tap into FanDuel’s sportsbook user base. The cross-selling potential strengthens its position against competitors like WSOP, BetRivers, and BetMGM. FanDuel's entry into online poker increases competition in the US market.

## What Happens Next

PokerStars Rewards will end on March 13, signaling a possible quick rollout of the new integration. Casino progressive jackpots will cease on April 12, further indicating imminent changes. An exact launch date remains undisclosed, but these timelines suggest a swift transition.

Here is the bottom line: FanDuel's strategic move to integrate with PokerStars not only expands its market reach but also enhances the player experience with larger, more competitive tournaments.
