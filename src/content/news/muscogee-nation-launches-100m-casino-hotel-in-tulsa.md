# Muscogee Nation Launches $100m Casino-Hotel in Tulsa

The Muscogee (Creek) Nation has unveiled a new $100 million casino-hotel in Tulsa, Oklahoma. The Coweta Casino Hotel, named after the Tulsa suburb where it is located, replaces the tribe's former Coweta casino. This development is managed by Muscogee Nation Gaming Enterprises (MNGE) and was marked by a ceremonial ribbon-cutting.

## The Numbers

The construction phase of the Coweta Casino Hotel supported approximately 501 jobs, generating an income of $22.6 million. According to Indian Gaming, the total economic impact reached $132.5 million. Post-opening, the casino-hotel has created 596 direct and indirect jobs, contributing $27.5 million in new annual income.

## Regulatory Context

The Muscogee Nation operates under federal and state gaming regulations, similar to other tribal casinos in Oklahoma. This ensures compliance with the Indian Gaming Regulatory Act, which governs tribal gaming activities.

## Market Impact

The new casino-hotel intensifies competition in Oklahoma's crowded tribal gaming market. The Muscogee Nation's strategic investment aims to rival existing operators like the Cherokee and Choctaw tribes. MNGE COO Andy Langston emphasized the tribe's purposeful expansion into Coweta, suggesting confidence in their competitive strategy.

## Player Impact

For players, the casino offers a 35,000 square-foot gaming area featuring 750 state-of-the-art gaming machines. This facility promises an enhanced gaming experience compared to the former Coweta casino.

## What Happens Next

The Muscogee Nation plans to leverage this investment to boost tourism and local business growth. Coweta Mayor Naomi Hogue and Tulsa Regional Tourism officials have expressed optimism about the economic partnership with the tribe. 

Here is the bottom line: The Coweta Casino Hotel is not just a gaming venue; it's a significant economic catalyst for the Tulsa region.
