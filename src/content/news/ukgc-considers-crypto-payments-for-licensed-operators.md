# UKGC Considers Crypto Payments for Licensed Operators

The UK Gambling Commission (UKGC) is evaluating the inclusion of cryptocurrency payments for licensed operators. This exploration aligns with the UK's 2027 cryptoasset regulatory framework, as outlined by UKGC Executive Director Tim Miller. He emphasized that this is a "tentative first step" towards integrating crypto payments within the existing licensing structure.

## Regulatory Context

The UKGC's inquiry into crypto payments coincides with the upcoming Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (Cryptoassets) Regulations 2025. This legislation will allow gambling operators to apply to the Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) to use crypto. The UK is preparing for a comprehensive crypto regime launch by October 2027, pending Parliamentary approval.

## Market Impact

Miller highlighted a growing interest from bettors in using digital currencies. He acknowledged that crypto-related searches are a primary factor driving UK gamblers to illegal sites. By potentially permitting crypto payments, the UKGC aims to curb the illegal market's growth and protect consumers through regulated innovation.

## The Numbers

Crypto-related searches are among the top drivers leading British gamblers to illegal sites, according to Miller. The UKGC’s move to explore crypto payments is partly a response to this data, indicating significant demand for digital currency transactions in the gambling sector.

## What Happens Next

The UKGC has established an industry forum to assess whether crypto payments can be developed responsibly within current licensing objectives. The forum's findings will influence whether licensed operators can eventually accept crypto payments, aligning with the anticipated 2027 regulatory framework.

## Key Facts

- UKGC is exploring crypto payments for licensed operators.
- The Financial Services and Markets Act 2000 (Cryptoassets) Regulations 2025 will guide this process.
- UKGC's crypto exploration is due to increased bettor interest.
- A new crypto regime is expected in October 2027, subject to Parliamentary approval.

Here is the bottom line: The UKGC's consideration of crypto payments could reshape the gambling landscape by integrating digital currency within a regulated framework, aiming to enhance consumer protection and limit illegal market growth.
